Admissions Policy:
- Applications for new students are accepted throughout the year, with main intakes in September and January.
- Required documents include: student passport copy, Emirates ID (if available), previous two years’ school reports, transfer certificate, and vaccination records.
- Priority for available seats is given to siblings of existing students, children of staff, and internal transfers between campuses.
- Entrance assessments may be required for core subjects such as English and Mathematics for Grades 2 and above.
- Final admission decisions are made by the Principal in consultation with the Admissions and Academic teams.

Fee and Payment Policy:
- Tuition fees are charged on a term-wise basis and must be paid before the first day of each term.
- Accepted payment methods include bank transfer, online payment portal, cheque, and in-person payments at the Accounts Office.
- A late fee may be applied if tuition fees are not settled within 14 days of the due date.
- Transport, uniforms, and extracurricular activities are billed separately from tuition fees.
- All refund requests must be submitted in writing to the Accounts Office and are processed in line with the school’s refund schedule.

Student Attendance and Leave Policy:
- Students are expected to maintain a minimum of 90% attendance per academic term to be promoted to the next grade.
- Planned leave (such as travel) must be requested in advance through the parent portal or via email to the class teacher and section head.
- Absences due to illness of more than two consecutive days require a medical certificate uploaded to the school portal or submitted to the class teacher.
- Repeated unexplained absences will be escalated to the Section Head and may require a meeting with parents.
- Students are responsible for catching up on any missed classwork or assessments during their absence.

Staff Leave and HR Policy:
- Full-time teaching staff receive annual leave as per their employment contract, aligned primarily with school holiday periods.
- Sick leave must be reported to HR and the Head of Department before 7:30 AM on the day of absence, preferably via email or the HR system.
- Emergency leave requests should be communicated to HR and the Principal as soon as possible and will be approved at the discretion of the school leadership.
- Staff are required to record their attendance and leave using the designated HR system or biometric device.
- Repeated unauthorized absences or late arrivals may trigger a formal HR review in line with the disciplinary policy.

Code of Conduct and Communication Policy:
- All communication with parents should be through official school channels, such as the school email address, parent portal, or approved messaging platforms.
- Teachers should acknowledge parent emails within one working day and provide a full response within three working days where possible.
- Sensitive issues involving student wellbeing, safeguarding, or serious complaints must be escalated to the Section Head, Counsellor, or Principal immediately.
- Staff are expected to maintain professional behaviour and confidentiality at all times, in accordance with the school’s safeguarding and data protection guidelines.
- Any incident of bullying, discrimination, or harassment must be documented and reported according to the school’s safeguarding procedures.

Transport Policy:
- School transport is available on designated routes and is subject to seat availability each term.
- Parents must register for transport services through the transport office or school portal before the start of term.
- Students are expected to follow the bus rules, including remaining seated, wearing seat belts where provided, and following the instructions of the bus attendant.
- Repeated misconduct on the bus may result in suspension of transport privileges after written warnings to parents.
- Changes to pick-up or drop-off points must be requested in writing at least two working days in advance.
